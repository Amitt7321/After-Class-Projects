Facts About Lions

Lions are highly social, apex predators—unique among cats for living in groups called prides—primarily inhabiting African grasslands, with a small population in India's Gir Forest. Known for their immense strength, males possess dark manes for protection and dominance, while females do most of the hunting. They are resting for up to 20 hours a day.

Lions are often seen as powerful, fearless rulers of the wild — but even the mighty have their weaknesses. And for lions, one of their biggest challenges is being alone.
